# Five AI Fracture Variables

**Author:** Alex Wu Roca  
**Thesis:** Organizational Intelligence Thesis  
**Version:** v0.1 canonical draft

---

## Core Claim

AI first fractures the Capability Shell of the firm.

It does not immediately eliminate the firm.

It changes the relationship between organizational capability and the traditional boundaries of the firm.

The five fracture variables are:

1. Intelligence Assets
2. Capability Generation Boundary
3. Capability Sedimentation Mechanism
4. Capability Invocation Mechanism
5. Capability Replication Mechanism

---

# 1. Intelligence Assets

## Definition

Intelligence Assets are organizational capabilities that become callable, trainable, replicable, and controllable through AI-mediated systems.

They include more than data.

They include:

1. Judgment patterns
2. Decision pathways
3. Workflow knowledge
4. Role-specific reasoning
5. Interaction histories
6. Feedback loops
7. Escalation rules
8. Context-specific heuristics
9. Organizational memory
10. System-embedded experience

## Why It Fractures the Firm

The traditional firm assumed that capability largely remained attached to people, roles, routines, and internal processes.

AI allows parts of that capability to become system-embedded.

Once capability becomes an intelligence asset, it can be trained, invoked, replicated, and controlled differently.

---

# 2. Capability Generation Boundary

## Definition

Capability Generation Boundary refers to where organizational capability is produced.

## Before AI

Capability was mainly generated through:

1. Hiring
2. Training
3. Internal work
4. Management
5. Apprenticeship
6. Organizational experience
7. Team routines

The firm was the default birthplace of capability.

## After AI

Capability can be generated through:

1. Human-system interaction
2. External models
3. Vendor platforms
4. AI agents
5. Shared workflows
6. Distributed knowledge systems
7. Cross-boundary feedback loops

## Fracture

The birthplace of capability changes.

The firm no longer has a monopoly over capability generation.

---

# 3. Capability Sedimentation Mechanism

## Definition

Capability Sedimentation Mechanism refers to how organizational experience becomes stored and retained.

## Before AI

Experience sedimented into:

1. People
2. Teams
3. Documents
4. Processes
5. Culture
6. Training systems
7. Managerial routines

## After AI

Experience can sediment into:

1. Prompts
2. Agents
3. Knowledge bases
4. Workflow systems
5. Decision records
6. Model feedback loops
7. Interface patterns
8. System-generated recommendations
9. Human-AI interaction histories

## Fracture

The storage location of capability changes.

Experience no longer remains only in people or traditional organizational memory.

---

# 4. Capability Invocation Mechanism

## Definition

Capability Invocation Mechanism refers to how organizational capability is called into action.

## Before AI

Capability was invoked through:

1. Managers
2. Experts
3. Departments
4. Standard operating procedures
5. Meetings
6. Approval chains
7. Manual coordination

## After AI

Capability can be invoked through:

1. AI interfaces
2. Agents
3. Automated workflows
4. Decision-support systems
5. Permission systems
6. Embedded recommendations
7. Human-system command structures

## Fracture

The switch of capability changes.

Whoever controls the invocation interface may control the practical use of organizational capability.

---

# 5. Capability Replication Mechanism

## Definition

Capability Replication Mechanism refers to how organizational capability is reproduced across people, teams, locations, and contexts.

## Before AI

Firms replicated capability through:

1. Hiring similar people
2. Training programs
3. Standardized procedures
4. Management oversight
5. Cultural transmission
6. Documentation
7. Promotion systems

## After AI

Firms can replicate:

1. Judgment structures
2. Decision paths
3. Workflow modules
4. Expert reasoning
5. Agent behaviors
6. Interface-guided action
7. Minimum capability baselines
8. System-embedded operating patterns

## Fracture

The unit of replication changes.

The firm no longer only replicates people.

It can replicate parts of judgment and capability structure.

---

## System-Level Implication

The five variables together show why AI changes the firm at a structural level.

AI changes:

1. What capability becomes
2. Where capability is born
3. Where capability is stored
4. How capability is invoked
5. How capability is copied
6. Who controls the resulting organizational intelligence

This is why the AI question is not merely a productivity question.

It is a control-rights question.

---

## Final Claim

The changing boundary of the firm is ultimately the changing boundary of organizational intelligence.
