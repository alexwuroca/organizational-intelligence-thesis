**Book Project:** What Is the Firm After AI?

---

## The Question

This thesis asks one central question:

When organizational capability can be designed, trained, invoked, replicated, and controlled outside traditional firm boundaries, why should the firm remain the default container for complex coordination?

---

## What This Thesis Does Not Argue

This thesis does not argue that firms will disappear.

It does not argue that AI is merely a productivity tool.

It does not argue that all organizations are the same as firms.

It does not argue that technology alone determines institutional structure.

It does not argue that responsibility can become fully distributed without consequence.

---

## Core Claim

The firm became the default modern organizational form because it enclosed complex coordination capability inside a stable structure of responsibility, authority, and capability.

The firm did not win only because it was efficient.

It won because it could make complex coordination:

1. Assignable
2. Governable
3. Accountable
4. Repeatable
5. Scalable
6. Socially recognizable

AI changes this arrangement.

It changes not only the speed of work, but the location and control structure of organizational capability.

---

## The Three Shells of the Firm

The traditional firm enclosed complex coordination capability through three shells.

### 1. Responsibility Shell

The Responsibility Shell answers:

Who bears the consequences?

It makes organizational action identifiable, accountable, regulatable, and socially processable.

### 2. Authority Shell

The Authority Shell answers:

Who decides when people disagree?

It turns dispersed judgment into unified action.

### 3. Capability Shell

The Capability Shell answers:

Where does capability remain when individuals leave?

It stores, stabilizes, invokes, and replicates organizational capability.

---

## What AI Fractures First

AI does not first destroy the firm.

AI first fractures the Capability Shell.

It changes:

1. Where capability is generated
2. Where capability is stored
3. How capability is invoked
4. How capability is replicated
5. Who controls the resulting intelligence assets

This is the central structural break.

---

## The New Object of Control

After AI, the most important control object is no longer only labor, capital, data, software, or process.

The new control object is Organizational Intelligence.

Organizational Intelligence includes the system mechanism through which an organization senses change, interprets meaning, mobilizes resources, coordinates action, receives feedback, trains itself, and replicates capability.

When Organizational Intelligence becomes embedded in AI-mediated systems, it becomes a strategic asset and a control-rights problem.

---

## The Control Rights Problem

Organizational Intelligence Control Rights include the rights to:

1. Define
2. Train
3. Access
4. Invoke
5. Modify
6. Replicate
7. Transfer
8. Audit
9. Assign responsibility

The key question becomes:

Who controls the organizational intelligence produced through AI-mediated work?

---

## Final Definition

After AI, the firm will shift from:

A legal organization that hires people, manages processes, and owns resources

to:

A responsibility-authority-capability structure that designs, trains, and controls organizational intelligence assets.

---

## Final Sentence

AI after the firm does not mean the end of firms.

It means the firm must be redefined around the control of organizational intelligence and the responsibility for its consequences.
