Core Question

What is the firm after AI?

The old question was why the firm exists.

After AI, the deeper question is what the firm becomes when complex coordination capability can be generated, accumulated, invoked, replicated, and controlled beyond the traditional boundaries of the firm.

The firm should be understood as a structure for stabilizing, controlling, reproducing, and making accountable complex coordination capability.

This is not only a question about technology. It is a question about capability, control, value, responsibility, and institutional stability.

Core Concepts

A structural vocabulary for AI-era organization.

The thesis is built around a small set of concepts designed to explain capability, control, value, and responsibility after AI.

Complex Coordination Capability

The capacity to organize judgment, coordination, execution, feedback, correction, and responsibility across multiple actors and systems.

Organizational Intelligence

The repeatable capacity of an organization to judge, act, receive feedback, correct itself, and bear responsibility over time.

Capability Shell

The structure that carries the generation, accumulation, invocation, replication, and improvement of organizational capability.

Power Shell

The structure that determines who can invoke, modify, allocate, replicate, transfer, audit, or shut down key capabilities.

Responsibility Shell

The structure that carries explanation, accountability, correction, remedy, legitimacy, and social trust.

Capability Attribution

The question of where the profit-producing capability actually resides.

Profit Quality

Whether profit is backed by capability that is sustainable, replicable, governable, auditable, and accountable.

Responsibility Capacity

The ability to explain, audit, correct, compensate, and remain legitimate when AI-amplified actions produce consequences.
