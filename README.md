## What This Repository Is

This repository is the canonical public source for the **Organizational Intelligence Thesis**.

The thesis examines how AI changes the generation, storage, invocation, replication, ownership, and control of organizational capability.

This is not a repository about AI tools.

It is a repository about how AI changes the structure of firms, responsibility, authority, capability, and control.

---

## Core Question

When organizational capability can be designed, trained, invoked, replicated, and controlled outside traditional firm boundaries, why should the firm remain the default container for complex coordination?

---

## Core Claim

The firm became the default modern organizational form because it enclosed complex coordination capability inside a stable structure of responsibility, authority, and capability.

After AI, what changes is not merely efficiency.

What changes is where organizational capability is generated, where it is stored, how it is invoked, how it is replicated, and who controls it.

Therefore, the changing boundary of the firm is ultimately the changing boundary of organizational intelligence.

---

## Final Definition

After AI, the firm will shift from:

> a legal organization that hires people, manages processes, and owns resources

to:

> a responsibility-authority-capability structure that designs, trains, and controls organizational intelligence assets.

## Why This Matters

The most dangerous misunderstanding of AI is to treat it merely as a tool of efficiency.

Efficiency explains speed.

It does not explain why capability changes location.

It does not explain why judgment moves from individuals into systems.

It does not explain why organizational experience can be stored, invoked, replicated, and controlled outside the people who originally produced it.

It does not explain why firms may lose control over the very capabilities they believe they are simply automating.

The central problem after AI is not:

> Who uses AI better?

The central problem is:

> Who controls the organizational intelligence produced through AI-mediated work?

The Organizational Intelligence Thesis argues that AI changes the structure of firms because it changes the way organizational capability is generated, stored, invoked, replicated, owned, and controlled.

In the industrial era, firms concentrated capability by hiring people, assigning roles, managing processes, and accumulating internal experience.

After AI, more and more organizational capability can be generated through human-system interaction, sedimented into intelligent assets, invoked through digital interfaces, replicated across contexts, and controlled through new rights structures.

This means the boundary of the firm is no longer only a legal, employment, or asset boundary.

It increasingly becomes a boundary of organizational intelligence.

The key question is no longer whether AI makes firms more efficient.

The key question is whether firms can still control the capabilities that make them firms.
