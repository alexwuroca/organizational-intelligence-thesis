# Core Concepts of the Organizational Intelligence Thesis

**Status:** Canonical v0.2.0  
**Author:** Alex Wu Roca  
**Release:** v0.2.0-two-books-canonical

---

## 1. Complex Coordination Capability

Complex Coordination Capability is the capacity to organize judgment, coordination, execution, feedback, correction, and responsibility across multiple actors, systems, processes, and constraints.

It is not merely productivity.

It is the deeper capability that allows complex work to be repeatedly organized, corrected, and made accountable over time.

---

## 2. Organizational Intelligence

Organizational Intelligence is the repeatable capacity of an organization to judge, act, receive feedback, correct itself, and bear responsibility over time.

It is not the intelligence of one individual.

It is not merely an AI model.

It is the capability structure produced by people, processes, data, models, feedback loops, authority, and responsibility.

---

## 3. Capability Shell

A Capability Shell is the structure that carries the generation, accumulation, invocation, replication, and improvement of organizational capability.

The key question is:

> Where is the capability actually generated and accumulated?

A firm may produce profit while the capability behind that profit is increasingly generated elsewhere.

---

## 4. Power Shell

A Power Shell is the structure that determines who can invoke, modify, allocate, replicate, transfer, audit, or shut down key organizational capabilities.

The key question is:

> Who can actually control the capability?

After AI, power may reside in model access, platform dependency, workflow control, data feedback, vendor systems, or operational permissions.

---

## 5. Responsibility Shell

A Responsibility Shell is the structure that carries explanation, accountability, correction, remedy, legitimacy, and social trust.

The key question is:

> Who remains accountable when AI-amplified capability produces consequences?

AI can amplify capability, but it does not automatically create responsibility.

---

## 6. Organizational Intelligence Control Rights

Organizational Intelligence Control Rights are the rights and capacities to train, invoke, modify, replicate, transfer, shut down, audit, and assign responsibility for organizational intelligence.

The deeper question is not only who owns data.

The deeper question is:

> Who controls the organizational intelligence produced through data, models, processes, feedback, and responsibility structures?

---

## 7. Capability Attribution

Capability Attribution asks where the key capabilities behind profit actually reside.

They may reside inside the firm, in individuals, vendors, AI platforms, customer networks, data-feedback loops, or hybrid structures.

This is a value interpretation question, not a direct legal ownership conclusion.

---

## 8. Profit Quality

Profit Quality refers to whether profit is generated by a capability structure that is sustainable, replicable, governable, auditable, and accountable.

Profit may remain on the company’s books.

But that does not prove the capability behind the profit remains inside the firm.

---

## 9. Feedback Loop Asset

A Feedback Loop Asset is an accumulative capability asset formed through repeated cycles of action, feedback, correction, and renewed action.

The key issue is not merely whether data exists.

The key issue is whether feedback returns to the organization and strengthens future capability.

---

## 10. Responsibility Capacity

Responsibility Capacity is the ability of an organization to explain, audit, correct, compensate, and remain legitimate when its actions produce consequences.

As AI amplifies capability, responsibility becomes more structurally important, not less.
