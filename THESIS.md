## 1. Core Thesis

AI does not merely improve efficiency. It changes where organizational capability is generated, accumulated, invoked, replicated, controlled, valued, and made accountable.

The Organizational Intelligence Thesis argues that the central question after AI is not simply whether firms become more productive, but whether firms can still control the complex coordination capabilities on which their value, responsibility, and legitimacy depend.

---

## 2. The Core Question

The old question was:

> Why does the firm exist?

After AI, the deeper question becomes:

> What is the firm when complex coordination capability can be generated, accumulated, invoked, replicated, and controlled outside the traditional boundaries of the firm?

This is not only a question about technology.

It is a question about capability, control, value, responsibility, and institutional stability.

---

## 3. Central Claim

A firm is not merely a legal entity, a bundle of contracts, or a production function.

A firm is a structure for stabilizing, controlling, reproducing, and making accountable complex coordination capability.

After AI, this structure must be re-examined because key capabilities may increasingly reside in models, platforms, vendors, workflows, data-feedback loops, employee-machine systems, customer networks, or hybrid arrangements that do not map cleanly onto the traditional firm boundary.

The firm may still hold the legal account.

But the capability may no longer remain fully inside the firm.

## 4. The First Two Completed Books

### Book 1  
## What Is the Firm After AI?

**Chinese title:** 《AI之后，企业是什么？》

Book 1 defines the firm as a structure for the stable control of complex coordination capability.

It argues that after AI, the firm can no longer be understood only as a legal container, contractual structure, or production unit. The deeper analytical object is the capability structure that allows complex work to be judged, coordinated, executed, corrected, repeated, and made accountable over time.

The core question is:

> What is the firm when organizational capability can be generated, accumulated, invoked, replicated, and controlled beyond the traditional firm boundary?

---

### Book 2  
## How Should Enterprise Value Be Reinterpreted After AI?

**Chinese title:** 《AI之后，企业价值如何被重新解释？》  
**Chinese subtitle:** 利润还在公司账上，不代表能力还在公司手里  
**English subtitle:** Profit may remain on the company’s books, but the capability may no longer remain inside the firm.

Book 2 extends the Organizational Intelligence Thesis into enterprise value.

It argues that after AI, enterprise value can no longer be interpreted only through reported profit.

The central question becomes:

> Profit may remain on the company’s books, but does the capability behind that profit still remain inside the firm?

This book argues that AI changes not only productivity, cost, and efficiency, but also the location of the capabilities that generate profit.

Profit may still be legally and financially recorded by the firm.

But the capability behind that profit may increasingly be generated, accumulated, invoked, improved, replicated, or controlled through:

- AI systems
- external platforms
- vendors
- models
- data-feedback loops
- customer networks
- individual experts
- hybrid workflows
- distributed responsibility structures

Therefore, enterprise value after AI must be interpreted through a new structural question:

> Does the firm still govern the capability that produces its profit?

## 5. Enterprise Value After AI

Traditional enterprise value analysis often begins with profit:

- How much profit does the firm generate?
- How fast is profit growing?
- How stable are margins?
- How durable is the business model?
- How defensible is the competitive position?

These questions remain useful.

But after AI, they are incomplete.

The deeper question is:

> What capability structure produced the profit?

A firm may show strong earnings while the underlying capability that creates those earnings has become less internal, less governable, less auditable, or less accountable.

The problem is not whether profit exists.

The problem is whether profit still reflects a capability structure that the firm can sustain, repeat, control, inspect, and take responsibility for.

---

## 6. Capability Attribution and Profit Quality

Book 2’s main claim is:

> Enterprise value after AI depends not only on profit level, but on profit quality.  
> Profit quality depends on capability attribution.

In more direct terms:

```text
AI changes capability formation.
Capability formation may move across firm boundaries.
When capability attribution becomes unclear, profit quality becomes harder to interpret.
When profit quality becomes harder to interpret, enterprise value must be reinterpreted.
```

Profit on the income statement does not necessarily mean capability remains inside the firm.

Capability Attribution asks where the profit-producing capability actually resides.

Profit Quality asks whether that profit is supported by a capability structure that is sustainable, replicable, governable, auditable, and accountable.

Therefore:

> Capability Attribution determines Profit Quality.

## 9. What This Thesis Is Not

This thesis is not:

- an AI tool guide
- a management trend book
- a short-term market prediction
- investment advice
- a claim that all firms will disappear
- a claim that AI automatically replaces human organization
- a claim that profit is fake
- a claim that legal ownership is irrelevant

It is a framework for asking a sharper structural question:

> Who controls the capability after AI, and who remains responsible for its consequences?
