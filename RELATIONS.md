# Conceptual Relations in the Organizational Intelligence Thesis

**Status:** Canonical v0.2.0  
**Author:** Alex Wu Roca  
**Release:** v0.2.0-two-books-canonical

---

## 1. Master Relation

AI does not merely improve efficiency. It changes where organizational capability is generated, accumulated, invoked, replicated, controlled, valued, and made accountable.

---

## 2. Capability Comes Before the Firm

The firm should not be analyzed only as a legal entity, production function, or bundle of contracts.

The deeper object is complex coordination capability.

A firm exists because complex coordination capability must be stabilized, controlled, repeated, and made accountable.

---

## 3. Organizational Intelligence Is the Repeatable Form of Capability

Complex coordination capability becomes organizational intelligence when it can be repeatedly used for judgment, action, feedback, correction, and responsibility.

Organizational intelligence is not a single model, database, employee, or process.

It is the repeatable capability structure produced by their interaction.

---

## 4. The Firm Is a Shell for Organizational Intelligence

A firm is one historical structure for holding organizational intelligence.

It can contain:

- a Capability Shell
- a Power Shell
- a Responsibility Shell

After AI, these shells may no longer align neatly.

Capability may be generated in one place.  
Power may be exercised in another place.  
Responsibility may remain legally assigned somewhere else.

This misalignment is the central structural problem.

---

## 5. Capability Shell Precedes Control Rights

Capability Shell precedes Organizational Intelligence Control Rights.

Without diagnosing where capability is generated and accumulated, control rights cannot be properly assigned.

Before asking who controls organizational intelligence, one must first ask:

> Where is the capability actually being formed?

---

## 6. Power Shell Determines Invocation and Control

The Power Shell determines who can invoke, modify, allocate, replicate, transfer, audit, or shut down organizational capability.

After AI, control may shift toward whoever controls:

- model access
- data feedback
- workflow permissions
- platform dependencies
- vendor systems
- operational interfaces
- training loops
- audit mechanisms

---

## 7. Responsibility Shell Determines Accountability

The Responsibility Shell determines who can explain, audit, correct, compensate, and remain legitimate when capability produces consequences.

As AI amplifies capability, responsibility does not disappear.

It becomes more structurally important.

---

## 8. Capability Attribution Determines Profit Quality

Profit on the income statement does not necessarily mean capability remains inside the firm.

Capability Attribution asks where the profit-producing capability actually resides.

Profit Quality asks whether that profit is supported by a capability structure that is sustainable, replicable, governable, auditable, and accountable.

Therefore:

> Capability Attribution determines Profit Quality.

---

## 9. Profit Quality Determines Enterprise Value Interpretation

Enterprise value after AI cannot be interpreted only through reported profit.

Profit quality depends on whether the profit-producing capability is durable, controllable, reproducible, inspectable, and accountable.

Therefore:

> When profit quality becomes harder to interpret, enterprise value must be reinterpreted.

---

## 10. Feedback Loops Determine Whether Capability Accumulates

AI may make an organization faster.

But speed is not enough.

The deeper question is whether feedback returns to the organization and becomes an asset.

If feedback is captured elsewhere, the firm may gain short-term efficiency while losing long-term capability accumulation.

---

## 11. Responsibility Capacity Determines Legitimacy

A capability structure without responsibility capacity is unstable.

A firm may increase capability through AI, but if it cannot explain, audit, correct, or bear responsibility for outcomes, its legitimacy becomes fragile.

---

---

## Non-Claims

This thesis does not claim that:

- all firms disappear
- all AI capability belongs to platforms
- legal ownership is irrelevant
- profit is fake
- markets cannot adapt
- responsibility is only a legal issue

It claims that AI makes existing categories insufficient unless we examine where capability is generated, controlled, valued, and made accountable.
