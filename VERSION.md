## Core Thesis Boundary

The Organizational Intelligence Thesis does not argue that firms disappear.

It does not treat AI as merely a productivity tool.

It does not claim that all organizations are the same as firms.

It focuses on how AI changes the generation, storage, invocation, replication, ownership, and control of organizational capability.

The central question is:

When organizational capability can be designed, trained, invoked, replicated, and controlled outside traditional firm boundaries, why should the firm remain the default container for complex coordination?

A responsibility-authority-capability structure that designs, trains, and controls organizational intelligence assets.
